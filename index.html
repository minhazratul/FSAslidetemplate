মজিলা

আমাদের মিশন হল ওয়েবের ওপেননেস, নতুনত্ব ও সুযোগকে মানুষের সামনে তুলে ধরা।
Mozilla project was created in 1998 with the release of the Netscape browser suite source code in order to “harness the creative power of thousands of programmers on the internet and fuel unprecedented levels of innovation in the browser market.” By being open, Mozilla was setting itself up to be larger than any one company and providing people around the world with different ways to get involved in the free software movement. Mozilla 1.0 was released in 2002 when over 90% of Internet users were on the web with Internet Explorer with the goal of “providing the best possible browsing experience to the widest possible set of people.” 2003 – Mozilla created the Mozilla Foundation to promote openness, innovation, and opportunity on the Internet. The Mozilla Foundation releases software (such as Firefox) and supports accessibility improvements on the Web.

Firefox Student Ambassadors

  • Students, teachers, and administrators
  • Passionate about Mozilla's Mission and Products
  • Leaders in the Mozilla Community
  • Champions for the Open Web
*Student Ambassadors are: Creative and resourceful, Student Ambassadors lead campaigns and projects at their colleges and in their communities to encourage others to contribute to Mozilla (and utilize our products). Together, Firefox Student Ambassadors play a large role in helping to improve the global experience of people on the Web. The program is open to those students 18+ as well as those individuals currently associated with a college or university (including professors and administrators) Individuals who are passionate about Mozilla Students, teachers, and administrators who want to raise awareness about the many benefits of using Firefox and other Mozilla products (not just limited to students) Leaders in the Mozilla Community and at their schools who are driving campaigns and projects on their campus to encourage others to contribute to Mozilla Over 7,000 creative and resourceful champions for the open web around the world.
