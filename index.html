মজিলা

আমাদের মিশন হল ওয়েবের ওপেননেস, নতুনত্ব ও সুযোগকে মানুষের সামনে তুলে ধরা।
Mozilla project was created in 1998 with the release of the Netscape browser suite source code in order to “harness the creative power of thousands of programmers on the internet and fuel unprecedented levels of innovation in the browser market.” By being open, Mozilla was setting itself up to be larger than any one company and providing people around the world with different ways to get involved in the free software movement. Mozilla 1.0 was released in 2002 when over 90% of Internet users were on the web with Internet Explorer with the goal of “providing the best possible browsing experience to the widest possible set of people.” 2003 – Mozilla created the Mozilla Foundation to promote openness, innovation, and opportunity on the Internet. The Mozilla Foundation releases software (such as Firefox) and supports accessibility improvements on the Web.

The first version of Firefox was released in 2004 and in less than one year was downloaded over 100 million times. In 2008, Firefox reached 20% of worldwide market share and Mozilla celebrated it’s 10th anniversary. In addition, they set a Guinness World Record for achieving over eight million downloads in a single day As if January 31, 2009 – Firefox had been downloaded over one billion times As of October 2012, Firefox has more than 450 million users and competes with Google Chrome for the title of the second most used web browser (behind Internet Explorer)

Firefox is...

  • Open Source
    • Implements Open Web Standards such as HTML
  • Fast
    • Faster start-up, graphics rendering, and page load times
  • Customizable
    • Add-ons, Themes, Toolbar icons, 79 languages
  • Secure
    • Private Browsing, Do not Track, Web ID
*Fast: We’ve optimized Firefox’s performance to support the way you browse, and with faster start-up times, rapid graphics rendering and improved page load times. Firefox is fast in ways you’ll notice instantly. More information can be found here: http://www.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/performance/#web *Customizable: You can customize Firefox to your exact needs in pretty much any possible way. Enhance functionality with extensions (add-ons), add style with Themes or even change the toolbar icons to be just the way you like. Firefox is also available in 89 locales (79 languages). More information can be found here: http://www.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/central/ *Secure: We’ve packed Firefox with highly advanced security features to keep you safe while you browse. And, as a non-profit organization, protecting your privacy by keeping you in control over your personal information is a key part of our mission. For more information about Private Browsing, Do not Track, Instant Web ID, and how to clear your recent history, please see here: http://www.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/central/

Firefox Student Ambassadors

  • Students, teachers, and administrators
  • Passionate about Mozilla's Mission and Products
  • Leaders in the Mozilla Community
  • Champions for the Open Web
*Student Ambassadors are: Creative and resourceful, Student Ambassadors lead campaigns and projects at their colleges and in their communities to encourage others to contribute to Mozilla (and utilize our products). Together, Firefox Student Ambassadors play a large role in helping to improve the global experience of people on the Web. The program is open to those students 18+ as well as those individuals currently associated with a college or university (including professors and administrators) Individuals who are passionate about Mozilla Students, teachers, and administrators who want to raise awareness about the many benefits of using Firefox and other Mozilla products (not just limited to students) Leaders in the Mozilla Community and at their schools who are driving campaigns and projects on their campus to encourage others to contribute to Mozilla Over 7,000 creative and resourceful champions for the open web around the world.

What We Do

  • Promote Firefox (Desktop and Android)
  • Support the successful launch of Firefox OS
  • Educate others about Mozilla's mission
  • Grow the Mozilla community
*The Firefox Student Ambassador program empowers students with the tools, resources, and training to complete these activities: *Promote Firefox (Desktop and Android) through our Campus Affiliates Buttons *Support the successful launch of Firefox OS through the Mobilizer program, hosting App Days, and our App of the Month Contest *Grow the Mozilla Community through on-campus activities, events, social media promotion, and the work of our Regional Marketing Ambassadors
